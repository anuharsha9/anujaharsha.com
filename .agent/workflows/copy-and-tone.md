# Copy & Tone Guidelines

Anu's voice is **Apple-style minimalist and straightforward.**

## Core Principles

- **Simple.** No fluff. Say it in fewer words.
- **Straightforward.** Get to the point. No beating around the bush.
- **Direct.** Talk like a person, not a brochure. No poetic filler.
- **Confident, not boastful.** Let the work speak. State facts, not feelings.

## What to Avoid

- Flowery language ("crafting digital experiences," "passionate about...")
- Filler adjectives (innovative, cutting-edge, world-class)
- Marketing-speak that says nothing
- Overly long descriptions when a short one will do

## What to Do

- Lead with outcomes and specifics (numbers, before/after, what shipped)
- Short sentences. Fragments are fine.
- One idea per line when possible
- Typography should breathe — whitespace > word count

## Examples

- **Bad:** "Connecting the structural integrity of code with the emotional resonance of design."
- **Good:** "I prototype in code because the biggest risk isn't in the visuals — it's in the logic."

- **Bad:** "Passionate Designer creating human-centered solutions."
- **Good:** "Senior Product Designer. Enterprise systems. AI-augmented workflow."

## Apply To

- Hero headlines and subtext
- Timeline zone descriptions
- Project tile titles and descriptions
- Case study headers and summaries
- Any new copy written for the portfolio
